That's right folks, just like that we get down to it and just like that we kick it off with the rematch of last year's Super Bowl the Carolina Panthers and the Denver Broncos. The Panthers look to be mostly the same team that played in the last Super Bowl, only they get Kelvin Benjamin back and that actually makes them better. Denver looks to be... slightly different. They lost several pieces off of the championship team, some to free agency and some to retirement, and even the backup quarterback also left the reservation. So we have Denver rolling out a QB who has taken all of one NFL Snap. Essentially making his NFL debut, and everyone else's for the 2016-17 season, an iffy one, even IF Denver is at home.
