Baltimore at Denver -9.5

Well Now that it counts for real, who is going to show up, and who is going to flame out. Denver for one is expecting big things on the year. Something that can be expected after the dominating run they went on toward the end of last year. Their lone blemish was when Baltimore came calling in the post season, and that right there was enough, to see ya next year. Well Next year is here, and we all had just about all of season to work towards this game and here at the last minute everything just went out the window. This one is going to be very difficult to handicap simply because the amount of points in play. Denver was my darling towards the end of last season, and well justified, as I hit on them repeatedly down the stretch. They also have a whole bunch working in their favor here, the whole home team opening night sort of thing, as well as just Denver's decided home advantage. Then figure in the well known and bet on Super bowl championship hangover... however I just don't buy all of that. This line is way over inflated for a Harbaugh coached team, especially a championship level team. So as we get set to mount our 2013 campaign we defy death, and take the points.

New England at Buffalo +10.5

The AFC East hits the ground running in this big time battle in Buffalo. On paper and at the betting windows this game does not look like much of a match up. Considering New England has partied plenty at Buffalo's expense where they have won a staggering 18 of 19 meetings with Buffalo, and covered in 8 of the last 9 games in Buffalo. However one would be remiss to overlook a big fat double-digit home dog even in the face of such a one sided rivalry. Buffalo ran out to a big lead at home last September, before New England hung 52 on them. Then Buffalo covered up in New England in November rematch. Usually a prodigious home team like Buffalo is healthy and despite being overmatched are coming in confidently. New England's defense was pretty porous last year, and they have had significant losses to their offense from last year. If nothing else this one looks like it could be a track meet.

Cincinnati at Chicago -2.5

A new season and new era kick off in Chicago where da Bears tangle with the cats in orange and black. While Chicago does have several pieces in tact from last year. Chicago faded losing 6 of their last 8 games and ultimately costing Lovie Smith his job. The new administration will look to turn that around against a guy who has his administration in line and a track record of success in Marvin Lewis and Cincinnati. Under Lewis the big cats are one well put together machine. I like Cincy in this game as they have beaten Chicago in 4 of the last 5 meetings, granted they don't play all that often, and this is a new season. Still Cincy ran off 4 wins in their last 5 road games, and we find them getting a bit of a boost fro the betting windows. However there is an old rule of thumb to never bet against the Bear In September. Well whatever.

Miami at Cleveland

The Miami mammal-fish swim their way up into the brown waters of Lake Erie to begin their campaign in Cleveland. One of the tougher match-ups on the day here and one I admittedly might not be completely sold on. I happen to really like Miami in the game. I know everyone has been raving about how improved the Cleveland offense is this season, but I don't know about how much of that is just hype against some uninterested teams in the preseason. I don't know if I believe the Cleveland offense will have much success against a pretty good Miami defense. Yet the Miami offense leaves much to be desired in terms of moving the ball consistently. Also Miami has been very sub-par on the road, only winning one of the last 5 road games. They have also been abysmal covering against Cleveland failing to cover at all in the last 5 contests against Cleveland. Yet Miami is now favored on the road getting all kinds of “wise guy” action. So you might understand my consternation, and reluctantly turning brown.

Atlanta at New Orleans -2.5

A juicy old southern shoot out getting the NFC South off to a high flying start. This here is a match up so good, were I of sound mind, I would be charging you people for this pick. At very least move it into the Prime Slot and make you work a little for it. New Orleans have managed to reign in the high flying Falcs in their match ups as New Orleans has won 4 of the last 5 meetings, and in New Orleans have come out on top in 6 of the last 7 years. Not surprising that the action is coming in hard and heavy on New Orleans down in the big easy. However nothing in the NFL is easy, and that right there gives me plenty of pause. There will be plenty on the line for both teams as New Orleans looks to reestablish itself as the apex predator in this dog eat dog division. Did I just say dog, as in under dog. As in I am taking the points.... eh, well on second thought, maybe I should just go along with the crowd and join in the party down on Bourbon street.

Tampa Bay at NY Jets +3.5

Ahh the jolly jesters... And the jovial men in green. Were this any sort of a competent team I'd would probably be all over this game with the green if you know what I mean. New York's defense is still pretty good, even if Revis is now going to be on the opposite sideline. Tennessee at Pittsburgh -7.5

Tennessee trudges to the tri-river city to try and put a dent in the men of steel. By the looks of action at the betting windows it appears as if it is not only me who is not giving Tenny a chance to do much of anything. In this here game though we are in the coverin business. Now last time these two met back in October of last year, Tennessee went and pulled the upset. That of course was as a 6 point home dog. They won't get nearly that kind of support up in three rivers and on opening day after all. Now Pittsburgh struggled somewhat this preseason, and people are saying they are going to be down a little this season. However Pittsburgh has been repeatedly reloading like that. Tennessee has also had their struggles in the preseason, as well as in regular season, last time they counted. Despite pulling off that Pittsburgh upset last year. While the action has been predominately Pittsburgh and while that sort of thing usually piques my interest, I'm pretty positive Pitt prevails.

Minnesota at Detroit -4.5

Another game here that I am not too all that crazy about. Even though by the looks of this one I should be. Minnesota marches into to Motown led by their big horse Adrian Peterson. Detroit is favored in this one, after a solid strong showing in the preseason, and probably by virtue of being at home. Yet Minnesota have had the best of their Motown mates in 8 of the last 11 games up in Detroit. Also covered in 5 of the last 6 meetings of purple and silver. Detroit flamed out last year dropping the last 8 straight, and have made several changes they feel good about. Minnesota finished last season strong winning their last 4 straight to get into the playoffs. In light of that I would think that Minnesota would be getting a little more respect from that. I might be tempted to run with the big horse. Detroit does have a lot of talent on offense and they do tend to start off the season strongly. Detroit may not be able to stop Peterson but they may be able to out score them there in their home debut. Maybe.

Oakland at Indianapolis -9.5

The Oakland Raiders roll into Indy to try their luck against Luck. Featuring another double digits opening week line. I can't remember any other time when there was this level of disparity in the opening week of a new season. At least since the advent of modern free agency. I know several fantasy sites are keen on picking up Terrelle Pryor here figuring that he would have a big day statistically speaking. Now that is the problem I have with fantasy football, even if I were to be playing it again, it clutters my judgment. I agree that Pryor might be a good fantasy add, and he may even have a big day, 80 rushing yards, a rushing TD, 180 yards through the air, and possibly another touchdown pass. See how that can taint your vision. Andrew Luck would be a far better add, yet he does not get mentioned cause some of us drafted him, and early, and against the Oakland defense will show you why.

IND 37 – OAK 20

Seattle at Carolina +4.5

The Carolina cats are at it again opening this season swatting at the Big Blue Bird. The cats are another dubious home dog. Only dubious in the fact that they should probably be a bigger home dog against a Seattle team that is clearly better on paper. Yet there are several experts out there extolling the virtues of Carolina, at least on Defense. We all know Cam Newton can be a dynamic playmaker, and if you read my dribble earlier on Oakland, the same could be said for Cam. I don't know if Carolina has enough elsewhere to stand up to Seattle. Seattle can be very stifling defensively, and they will be running the ball with Marshawn Lynch. Carolina may have a solid front seven, but their secondary is seriously suspect. Suspect also however is that Seattle really does not travel all that well. Traveling here to Carolina as a rather heavy road favorite are also a little dubious and you might be better off grabbing the cat and the points. I won't be going that way, but you might be better off.

Kansas City at Jacksonville +3.5

More cats in the act, this time disguised as Jaguars. Jacksonville may have absolutely elevated their game, and are now the worst dressed team in the NFL to go along with their play on the field. What on earth is with the two-tone helmets is that supposed to look good. I guess it will fit the bill when they eventually move to L.A., oh please don't give me any crap about the Sheik Abdullah Khan or whomever owns the team being committed to Jacksonville. I been to Jacksonville, trust me there is NOT enough money there to keep the sheik smiling, that two tone will look better with all the plastic people out in la-la-land. Speaking of the worst Kansas City spent the last few years trying to attain that title. To avoid earning that distinction K.C. made wholesale changes and brought in new coach Andy Reid looking to move up into the muddled middle of the pack up from the bottom of the basement. So in a slugfest among bottom dwellers whom should we turn to, the ugliest of course, the slugs, the catfish of the world, the bottom feeders. The successful predators in that environment are generally the ugliest, who is uglier than Jacksonville?

Arizona at St. Louis -4.5

That's right folks the Cards are in St Louis to kick off a new season. That's a statement that is probably really old, and only a really old guy would appreciate and even then it would be slightly slippery. If you are leaning at taking the points here you might want to look out for the little bird as they managed only one win on the road all last season. St Louis was only marginally better having pulled out only one home win in their last 5 home games. As divisional rivals Arizona and St Louis have some familiarity playing each other twice a year, and Arizona has feasted on that St. Louis barbecue (almost as if they belong there...) having beaten St. Louis in 7 of the last 8 meetings. An interesting matchup in the wild west the gateway to the west I somehow suspect St Louis to buck the trends and turn that around.
************************************
STL 24 – AZ 13
************************************

Green Bay at San Francisco -4.5

The classic battle of the bays. San Fran the defending conference champs kick off their campaign to get back to big dance. One of the obstacles to that is the team San Fran sent packing in the postseason on their way to the big dance. Green Bay comes in with more than a little something to prove. This is another one of those epic battles that we should be charging a premium for, at least on paper. Oh sure it looks like a great match up, but in reality these are two teams heading the opposite way. San Fran looks awfully good on both sides of the ball, Green Bay still has some questions on defense and on the offensive line. Green Bay does have enough though to go out there, and not only pull off the upset, they could even win this one convincingly. They will be looking to exact revenge, but the only exacting will be the result.

SF 34 – GB 27
